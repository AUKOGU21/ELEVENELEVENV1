ELEVENELEVEN is a decision intelligence layer for ecommerce.

It helps women make better purchase decisions before checkout by showing them outcomes from highly relevant peers.

The core problem is not lack of data. It is lack of relevant context.

Women do not trust size charts, model photos, or generic reviews. They want to know:

Does this work for someone like me
Will this actually fit my body
Is this worth buying or will I regret it

ELEVENELEVEN answers those questions by matching users to their closest mirrors and surfacing real decisions and outcomes.

The product is not a marketplace. It is not a review platform. It is a decision layer that sits on top of existing product pages.

---

## Core Insight

Most ecommerce friction happens at the moment of decision.

Users hesitate because they cannot predict outcomes.

Existing solutions provide:

1. More information
2. More reviews
3. More images

None of these solve the actual problem, which is:

I do not see someone like me making this decision successfully

ELEVENELEVEN solves this by providing matched peer outcomes instead of generic information.

## Product Concept

ELEVENELEVEN allows users to:

1. Post items they are considering buying
2. Share their uncertainty and confidence level
3. Receive input from matched users with similar body type, sizing, style, and preferences
4. Make a decision with higher confidence
5. Log the outcome after purchase

This creates a feedback loop of:

decision → outcome → future decision improvement

## Matching Algorithm

The match score between two users is a weighted composite from 0 to 100.

### Weights

1. Silhouette match — 30%
   Primary signal. Captures body shape in a way that generic size labels cannot.

2. Body proportions — 20%
   Includes chest, torso length, waist definition, hip width, shoulders, inseam, and thigh fit.
   Refines silhouette-based matching rather than replacing it.

3. Height similarity — 15%
   Important for proportions but should not outrank silhouette.

4. Fit preference — 15%
   Includes preference for fitted, relaxed, oversized, compressive, and similar fit behavior.

5. Size system alignment — 10%
   Generic size labels used as a support signal only.
   Size labels are too coarse and too brand-inconsistent to be the main predictor.

6. Style and aesthetic overlap — 5%
   Secondary to all fit and body signals.
   Matters, but only after fit relevance is established.

7. Behavioral similarity — 5%
   Includes purchase frequency, brand sensitivity, and return or keep behavior.

### Matching Logic

The system works in two layers:

Layer 1 — Candidate filtering
Use category, brand context, and basic body compatibility to narrow the pool.

Layer 2 — Weighted scoring
Rank candidates using the weights above.
Silhouette is the strongest predictor.
Size labels support but do not drive the score.
